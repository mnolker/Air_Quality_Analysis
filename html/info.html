About the Data

Welcome to the About Page!

This website was designed as a decision-making tool for anyone who is impacted by air pollution.

What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution is the release of small particles and gases into the air from car and factory emissions, dust, pollen, and mold spores that can harm human health. This website will provide the user with the following pollutants that can be harmful to the human respiratory system:

  • • pm 2.5 – From combustion or organic compounds with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers.
  • • pm 10 – Particulates of dust, pollen, and mold spores with a diameter of 10 micrometers.
  • • ozone – Gaseous molecule also known as smog.
  • • carbon monoxide – Gaseous molecule from car and factory emissions.
  • • sulfur dioxide – Gaseous molecule from car and factory emissions. Can contribute to acid rain.
  • • nitrogen dioxide – Gaseous molecule from car and factory emissions.

For more information, please visit the Environmental Protection Agency website.

What is the Air Quality Index (AQI)?

An index developed by the EPA for reporting air quality levels. The AQI is used for the six air pollutants mentioned above.

What is being displayed?

On the Home Screen, is a map of California and its counties. The markers are displaying the current and most abundant air pollutant at that reporting station. The markers are different colors to indicate the Index Value. More information is available by clicking on the marker.

The user will also be able to animate historical AQI Data by clicking the play button at the bottom of the screen.

Where did the data come from?

The air quality data was obtained by scraping the airnow.gov website.
